Anomaly Detection Data Challenge 2021

In this challenge, you will develop algorithms for detecting New Physics by reformulating the problem as an out-of-distribution detection task. Armed with four-vectors of the highest-momentum jets, electrons, and muons in a collision event, together with the missing transverse energy, the goal is to pick up New Physics signarures from the overwhelming Standard Model background, using anomaly detection.

The algorithms are intended for a "triggering" environment, meaning both resources and time is limited. Therefore only four-vectors are provided for the leading 10 jets, 4 muons, 4 electrons and the missing ET.

Further complicating things, is that trigger algorithms must have a very low latency, of around one microsecond, and in addition must be resource efficient. The task is therefore to find the architecture that maximises the sensitivity for New Physics, but at the lowest possible resource budget (computed in FLOPs). The winner will be the team with the highest true positive rate at a fixed false positive rate of 10E-5, roughly the SM suppression needed for a trigger algorithm, and the lowest possible number of FLOPs.

We provide a training sample consisting of SM events, a few New Physics test samples, as well as a "black box" test sample, containing a mixture of SM and and unknows New Physics signal... Each team will be asked to submit the list of events passing a selection on the algorithm as well as the total number of FLOPs for the architecture.
